// The addiction mechanism

The same neural circuitry that governs addiction to substances governs technology addiction. Intermittent variable reward â€” the unpredictable appearance of likes, messages, and novel content â€” is the most powerful conditioning schedule known in behavioral psychology. Slot machines use the same principle.

When you check your phone and find a notification, dopamine floods your striatum. When you don't, dopamine drops â€” creating an aversive state that compels you to check again. This creates a compulsive loop that operates below conscious intention, which is why "just checking" always becomes more.

// Why adolescents are uniquely vulnerable

Adolescent brains have a fully developed limbic system (emotion, reward, impulse) but an underdeveloped prefrontal cortex (regulation, long-term thinking, inhibition). This developmental mismatch means teens experience digital rewards more intensely and have less neurological capacity to resist them.

Critically, the prefrontal cortex completes development around age 25. Patterns formed during adolescence â€” including digital habits â€” become deeply encoded as default behavioral pathways. What you do now shapes the brain you'll have for the rest of your life.
