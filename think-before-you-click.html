How to Verify
Information Online

1
Pause Before You Share
Slow down. Ask yourself: "Do I actually know this is true, or does it just feel true?" Emotional impulse is misinformation's best friend.
2
Find the Original Source
Go directly to the primary source. If a health claim is mentioned, visit WHO.int or DOH.gov.ph to confirm it officially.
3
Cross-Reference Multiple Sources
One source is never enough. Check if at least 2‚Äì3 independent, credible news organizations are reporting the same story.
4
Use Fact-Checking Sites
Run the headline through Snopes, FactCheck.org, Vera Files, or TSEK.PH ‚Äî dedicated to debunking misinformation.
5
Reverse Image/Video Search
Drag an image into Google Images or use TinEye to see where it came from. Many viral images are old or altered.
6
Ask: Why Was This Made?
Consider the motivation. Is this to inform, or to make you angry and scared? Emotional manipulation is misinformation's core strategy.
